# HOME — Vision

*Every life, a story. Every story, a home.*

---

## What Home is

Our digital lives are scattered. HOME gives them a home.

**HOME is a Living Archive where every person can build a digital home for the story of their life.**

Each resident has a house. Inside it are rooms, and each room holds a part of who they are — their story, their photos, their music, their links to the wider online world. Visitors don't scroll a feed; they walk through a home. They knock, they enter, they look around, and they leave knowing a person a little better.

Home gives a person's digital life **continuity and meaning**, in one place, under their own roof.

## What Home is not

- **Not a social network.** There are no feeds, no likes, no algorithms competing for attention. Home is calm. Nothing here is designed to compete for a person's attention.
- **Not a content mill.** Nothing in Home exists to generate engagement. It exists to hold a life, gently and permanently.
- **Not owned by AI.** AI may help a resident organise, connect, and rediscover what they have chosen to keep — but it never defines the archive, never adds to it uninvited, and never owns its structure.

## The principles

**1. Rooms, not pages.**
A house is made of rooms, and every room is built on the same foundation — the Room Engine. A room can hold a story, a gallery, a link to the outside world, or something we haven't imagined yet. New kinds of rooms should fit naturally into the house, never feel bolted on.

**2. Doors to the wider world.**
Some rooms point outward — to Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, anywhere a resident's life already lives online. Home doesn't replace the internet; it gives it an address.

**3. Some things live here.**
Stories, photographs, and memories may live inside the house itself, where the owner chooses to keep them. What lives here belongs to the resident — always.

**4. Sharing is intentional.**
Every room has a door, and every door has a lock the owner controls. Public, private, or shared with chosen people — nothing is visible by accident. Curtains drawn means curtains drawn.

**5. Rooms remember each other.**
A life is connected: a story mentions a place, a place has photographs, photographs have people. Over time, rooms will be able to reference one another, forming a map of a life. AI may help discover these connections — the resident decides which connections become part of their archive.

**6. Build carefully on solid ground.**
Home grows by strengthening its foundation, never by tearing it down. Every change is small, deliberate, and tested. What already works is protected.

## The feeling

Arriving at your own house should feel like coming home — *"Welcome back."* — not like opening a dashboard. Visiting someone else's house should feel like being welcomed in, not like inspecting a profile.
